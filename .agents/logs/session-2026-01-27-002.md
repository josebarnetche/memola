## The Five Diagnostic Questions

New filter for MEMOLA fit:

### Q1: Can this entity survive long enough for structure to matter?

Structure takes time. If the entity faces existential risk in 12 months, structure is irrelevant.

**Test**: What's the minimum viable survival horizon?
- If < 2 years: MEMOLA may be premature
- If > 5 years: MEMOLA has room to work

### Q2: Does failure here look like erosion, not explosion?

Erosion: gradual loss of position, relevance, margin
Explosion: sudden collapse from single event

**Test**: What kills this entity?
- Erosion deaths: loss of talent, market share decay, technology obsolescence
- Explosion deaths: key customer loss, regulatory ban, founder departure

### Q3: Is legitimacy more valuable than speed in this market?

Some markets reward "first" (winner-take-all dynamics)
Some markets reward "trusted" (relationship dynamics)

**Test**: Would customers choose a 2-year-old trusted player over a 6-month-old faster one?
- If yes: legitimacy regime (MEMOLA fits)
- If no: speed regime (MEMOLA may not fit)

### Q4: Will increased clarity actually change outcomes?

MEMOLA produces clarity. But clarity only matters if decisions can change.

**Test**: Is the bottleneck information or execution?
- If information: clarity helps
- If execution: clarity is necessary but not sufficient

### Q5: If nothing 'goes viral', does this still make sense?

Viral growth is a specific regime (network effects, consumer, attention economy).
Many businesses don't need viral — they need reliable.

**Test**: What's the growth model?
- Viral: consumer apps, social products, content
- Reliable: B2B, enterprise, infrastructure, institutions

## The Terrain Metaphor

> "MEMOLA is not building foundations. It is choosing terrain where foundations still matter."

This is the key insight.

### Foundations vs. Sprints

**Foundations**: Durable structures; expensive to build; last generations; require stable terrain
**Camps**: Mobile structures; cheap to build; easily abandoned; work on any terrain

MEMOLA builds foundations. The question isn't "Is this a good foundation?"
The question is "Is this terrain where foundations make sense?"

### What Terrain Suits Foundations?

- **Stable ground**: Markets that don't shift overnight
- **Defensible position**: Moats that compound over time
- **Resource access**: Sustainable economics, not boom-bust
- **Strategic location**: Where being present matters more than being fast

### What Terrain Suits Camps?

- **Shifting ground**: Markets evolving rapidly
- **No defensible position**: Winner-take-all dynamics
- **Resource scarcity**: Burn now, figure it out later
- **Anywhere is fine**: Speed to anywhere beats position at somewhere
