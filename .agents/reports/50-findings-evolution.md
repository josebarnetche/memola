## TERRAIN CLASSIFICATION

### 6. Castle vs Camp

**First Thought**: All businesses need structure. MEMOLA helps build structure.

**Now Thinks**: Two terrains exist — **castle** (slow erosion, trust compounds) and **camp** (sudden death, speed wins). MEMOLA is castle doctrine. Camp terrain needs different tools.

**Shift**: New understanding

---

### 7. Failure Modes

**First Thought**: Failure is failure. MEMOLA helps prevent it.

**Now Thinks**: Failure has two modes — **erosion** (gradual decline) and **explosion** (sudden collapse). MEMOLA prevents erosion. It cannot prevent explosion in explosion-prone terrain.

**Shift**: New understanding

---

### 8. The Five Questions

**First Thought**: Client qualification is about budget and scope.

**Now Thinks**: Five diagnostic questions gate every engagement: survival horizon, failure mode, legitimacy vs speed, clarity impact, virality independence. Score below 4/5 = decline.

**Shift**: New framework

---

### 9. Legitimacy vs Speed

**First Thought**: Both matter. Balance them.

**Now Thinks**: In castle terrain, legitimacy > speed. In camp terrain, speed > legitimacy. You cannot serve both. Choose your terrain.

**Shift**: Refinement

---

### 10. Virality Requirement

**First Thought**: Viral growth is a bonus for any business.

**Now Thinks**: If virality is **required** for survival, MEMOLA is wrong tool. MEMOLA works where success is possible without going viral.

**Shift**: New criterion

## THE FIVE PRINCIPLES

### 11. Principle Sequence

**First Thought**: The five principles are guidelines to keep in mind.

**Now Thinks**: The sequence IS the method. Reality → Structure → Systems → Assets → Compounding. Skip a step and the whole thing fails.

**Shift**: Refinement

### 12. Reality Before Narrative

**First Thought**: Understand the client before positioning them.

**Now Thinks**: This applies to MEMOLA itself. Narrative before reality (like the Miami expansion) violates doctrine even when it's MEMOLA doing it.

**Shift**: Self-application

---

### 13. Structure Before Execution

**First Thought**: Plan before you act.

**Now Thinks**: Structure is not just planning — it's creating the architecture that makes execution meaningful. Execution without structure is motion without progress.

**Shift**: Deepening

---

### 14. Systems Before Tactics

**First Thought**: Think strategically, not tactically.

**Now Thinks**: Build systems that produce outcomes repeatedly. Tactics are one-time. Systems compound. A campaign is a tactic. A brand is a system.

**Shift**: Clarification

---

### 15. Assets Before Activity

**First Thought**: Create value before marketing it.

**Now Thinks**: Assets are containers that hold and compound value. Activity fills containers. Building containers before having things to put in them = wasted structure. Having things with no container = value leakage.

**Shift**: Deepening

---

### 16. Compounding Before Scale

**First Thought**: Grow sustainably.

**Now Thinks**: Depth before breadth. A deep relationship with 100 people beats a shallow relationship with 10,000. Compounding requires concentration before distribution.

**Shift**: Clarification

## GOVERNANCE

### 17. Decision Authority

**First Thought**: Make sure someone can approve the work.

**Now Thinks**: Without clear decision authority, strategy becomes theater. "We'll need to check with..." is a red flag. Strong authority = proceed. Weak authority = caution. No authority = decline.

**Shift**: Hardening

---

### 18. Scope Creep

**First Thought**: Manage scope carefully.

**Now Thinks**: Scope creep is governance failure, not project management failure. The fix is structural (written scope, change process) not behavioral (saying no more often).

**Shift**: Reframing

---

### 19. Exit Conditions

**First Thought**: Know when to end engagements.

**Now Thinks**: Exit conditions must be defined **before entry**. Performance triggers, governance triggers, ethical triggers — all documented upfront. No exit conditions = don't enter.

**Shift**: Hardening

---

### 20. The Four Requirements

**First Thought**: Good governance is important.

**Now Thinks**: Four requirements, all mandatory: (1) Clear decision authority, (2) Defined scope, (3) Explicit ownership, (4) Exit conditions. Missing any one = do not proceed.

**Shift**: Systematization

---

## PLATFORMS

### 21. Platform Temptation

**First Thought**: Platforms are powerful. Build them when possible.

**Now Thinks**: Platform prematurity is a known failure mode. Most "should we build a platform?" questions should be answered "not yet" or "no."

**Shift**: Caution added

---

### 22. Manual First

**First Thought**: Technology enables scale.

**Now Thinks**: Phase 1 is always manual. Do the platform's job by hand first. If manual doesn't create value, technology won't either. Technology amplifies; it doesn't create.

**Shift**: New principle

### 23. Platform Viability Criteria

**Now Thinks**: Five criteria must score high: problem repetition, information fragmentation, aggregation leverage, niche definition, MEMOLA fit. Score below 9/15 = don't build.

**Shift**: Systematization

### 24. Service vs Platform

**Now Thinks**: Many things that look like platforms should stay services. The service → platform transition requires proven manual value, efficiency gains, and user willingness to self-serve.

**Shift**: Nuance added
